Built for local business reality

Not just pretty pages — the whole customer path needs to make sense.

A good local-business website should line up with your Google listing, your Apple Maps presence, your phone number, your service area, your forms, your reviews, and your actual offer. If any of those pieces are confusing or inconsistent, you lose trust and lose leads.

  • Clear service pages built around what customers search for
  • Calls-to-action designed for real businesses: call, quote request, form, visit, or appointment
  • Messaging that sounds like a real local business, not a generic template
  • Fast-loading static website structure that works well on GitHub Pages
